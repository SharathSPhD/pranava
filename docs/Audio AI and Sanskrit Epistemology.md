# **Epistemic Architectures and the Acoustic Continuum: Integrating Navya-Nyāya and Śabdādvaita into Multimodal Language Models**

The rapid evolution of artificial intelligence has historically been anchored in the processing of discrete, symbolic text tokens. While Large Language Models exhibit exceptional proficiency in syntactic and semantic pattern matching, they fundamentally operate upon pre-segmented data, stripping language of its continuous acoustic, prosodic, and embodied dimensions. Current generative paradigms assume a silent, static substrate devoid of the ambient, continuous presence of sound, thus modeling only "already parsed" language rather than the active cognitive process of parsing reality1. Conversely, profound insights derived from classical Sanskrit epistemology—specifically Śabdādvaita, the Vākyapadīya of Bhartṛhari, and the Navya-Nyāya logical framework—posit that awareness and sound are inextricably coupled. In these traditions, cognition is treated as a continuous field divided by meaning-evoking power rather than a mere sequence of static objects1.  
When combined with contemporary neuroscientific models of the human auditory cortex and the intricate network dynamics of inner speech, a profound theoretical gap in modern AI architectures becomes evident. Purely textual models lack the epistemic grounding, the continuous situational awareness, and the event-centric ontology that biological cognition utilizes to navigate the world3. The synthesis of Sanskrit darśana, the neuroscience of speech-centered cognition, and state-of-the-art continuous audio models presents a transformative blueprint for next-generation systems. The following exhaustive analysis explores the theoretical and architectural implications of bridging these domains, proposing a blueprint for Neuro-Symbolic Acoustic Models, and outlining a strategic regional knowledge base utilizing the academic ecosystems surrounding Bushey, Hertfordshire, and Central London to pioneer this interdisciplinary synthesis.

## **The Ontological Primacy of Sound and Awareness**

### **Śabdādvaita and the Vākyapadīya: The Continuous Word-Essence**

In the philosophical tradition of Śabdādvaita, or the non-dualism of the Word, ultimate reality is conceptualized as an eternal Word-essence. This framework was primarily articulated by the fifth-century grammarian-philosopher Bhartṛhari in his seminal text, the Vākyapadīya, which serves as a foundational treatise on grammar and its metaphysical underpinnings2. The text establishes that the imperishable essence of reality manifests as the process of the world strictly through the arising of meaning, a concept known as artha-bhāva1. This perspective aligns closely with Kashmir Śaiva darśana, effectively mapping the relationship between Word and Meaning to the continuous, undifferentiated interplay of awareness and manifestation1.  
The Vākyapadīya delineates this philosophy through its opening verses, establishing a rigorous ontology of sound. The first verse asserts that the imperishable essence of the beginning-less and end-less reality is the Word itself, manifesting the world through the continuous evocation of meaning1. The second verse explains that this singular reality becomes manifold through its inherent power, which causes differentiation despite the underlying unity1. The third verse introduces the critical concept of krama, or the power of time. Time is characterized as an inherent tendency of the Word that sustains the six types of actions—beginning, being, changing, growing, decaying, and ending1. These actions, which form the semantic core of verbs, act as the wombs for manifold objects, which form the semantic core of nouns. This establishes a strictly verb-first ontology where processes and actions precede static objects, directly challenging the object-centric architectures of modern computing1.  
Within this framework, human perception is underpinned by an uninterrupted background continuum of sound1. Meaning occurs when the mind segments this acoustic continuum into discrete, intelligible units. Bhartṛhari’s Sphoṭa theory explicitly distinguishes between the physical acoustic vibration, known as dhvani or nāda, and the invariant, meaning-bearing essence of the word, known as the Sphoṭa7. The Sphoṭa is an indivisible, sequence-less unit of cognition that flashes into awareness, whereas dhvani is merely the sequential auditory vehicle used to manifest it8. Consequently, language and reality are understood as processual, action-oriented, and inherently continuous phenomena, rendering the artificial extraction of parts an exercise that serves pedagogical purposes but obscures the holistic nature of comprehension1.

### **The Neuroscience of Inner Speech and Acoustic Cognition**

The philosophical postulation of a continuous cognitive-acoustic field finds striking empirical parallels in the modern neuroscience of inner speech and auditory processing. Inner speech, defined as the subjective experience of silent verbal thought, is not merely the subvocal rehearsal of phonological utterances. It is the cornerstone of autobiographical memory, executive function, and the integration of internal narratives10. The phenomenon of inner speech acts as a cognitive anchor, integrating sensory realities with semantic memory.  
Recent neuroimaging studies utilizing seed-based co-activation pattern analyses and dynamic conditional correlation have identified a complex neural architecture governing deliberate, situationally simulated inner speech4. Unlike the simple mental imagery of isolated words, situationally simulated inner speech involves the active and dynamic integration of contextual background, episodic memories, and external events into a coherent narrative structure4. This process requires the dynamic coordination of three large-scale brain networks, forming a triple-network model.

| Neural Network | Anatomical Correlates | Function in Inner Speech |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Language Network (LAN)** | Inferior frontal gyrus, superior temporal gyrus | Processes a truncated form of overt speech and handles symbolic, lexical-phonological structuring4. |
| **Sensorimotor Network (SMN)** | Bilateral somatosensory areas, precentral/postcentral gyri | Facilitates the perceptual simulation of articulation and the continuous monitoring of the inner voice4. |
| **Default Mode Network (DMN)** | Medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate | Responsible for the integration of episodic memory, contextual background, and high-level autobiographical sense-making4. |

Furthermore, the processing of continuous acoustic speech relies on hierarchical predictive coding within the auditory cortex. Neural activity dynamically organizes into distinct oscillatory timescales to optimize sensory sampling. Fast sensory sampling occurs in the theta band (4–10 Hz), tracking contextual uncertainty and phonemic inputs in early auditory regions3. Simultaneously, slower downstream responses in the delta band (0.5–4 Hz) track prediction errors and surprising semantic inputs3. Much like the Sphoṭa emerging from the continuous dhvani, the biological brain continuously parses multi-timescale acoustic streams to extract an indivisible semantic realization, actively computing the difference between predicted and actual speech sounds to minimize prediction error3.

### **The Architectural Chasm in Text-Only Language Models**

Evaluated against the paradigms of Śabdādvaita and modern neuroscience, standard text-only Large Language Models present severe architectural limitations. Textual models operate exclusively on pre-chunked, discrete sub-word tokens, effectively assuming a silent substrate devoid of the ambient, continuous presence of sound1. By modeling only language that has already been parsed, these systems bypass the cognitive act of segmenting the continuum into meaningful units.  
Furthermore, text models operate in a noun-first paradigm optimized for static propositional logic and object-attribute relationships. They discard the temporal krama and the verb-centric ontology that is central to human cognition, wherein actions give rise to objects1. While these models possess structural homologies to the human Language Network, demonstrating exceptional sequential and syntactic patterning, they completely lack the embodied simulation provided by the Sensorimotor Network and the continuous, context-integrated awareness maintained by the Default Mode Network4. Consequently, they do not parse reality as an unfolding event stream; they compute statistical correlations over isolated symbols abstracted far away from the acoustic reality that originally generated them1.

## **Navigating the Acoustic Continuum in Multimodal AI**

To capture the deeply coupled nature of sound and awareness, AI architectures must transition from pipelined systems—where automatic speech recognition feeds into a text model, which then feeds into text-to-speech—toward natively continuous, event-centric audio models20. The traditional three-stage pipeline inevitably loses critical paralinguistic information, prosody, timing, and overlapping multi-speaker cues22.

### **Bridging the Acoustic-Semantic Divide**

Early approaches to audio tokenization utilized discrete vector quantization, compressing continuous audio into semantic codes akin to text tokens6. While discrete tokens allow audio to be treated mathematically like text, they suffer from acoustic blindness. This highly compressed abstraction discards fine-grained paralinguistic cues, emotional resonance, prosody, and the realities of overlapping acoustic environments6. As emerging research highlights, the representation termed "semantic" in discrete speech tokenizers rarely aligns perfectly with text-derived semantics, leading to a modality gap that degrades the reasoning capabilities of multimodal models6.  
Recent architectural breakthroughs have shifted focus toward continuous acoustic embeddings and dual-head transformer designs. Models such as the Autoregressive Diffusion Transformer and the VibeVoice Realtime architecture replace highly compressed discrete tokens with continuous speech tokenization paired with next-token diffusion processes20.  
The dual-head architecture serves as a critical mechanism for preserving both logical coherence and acoustic fidelity. In systems like Ming-UniAudio, the standard language modeling head predicts discrete semantic tokens, ensuring logical task completion and determining the semantic endpoints of an utterance. Simultaneously, a separate diffusion head operates frame-by-frame, generating continuous speech embeddings conditioned on the hidden states of the language model to synthesize high-fidelity waveforms20. This reconciles the competing requirements of speech representation: understanding tasks demand compact, semantic encodings, while generative tasks demand rich, continuous acoustic details27.  
Innovations in low-frame-rate acoustic tokenization further enable the processing of the continuous acoustic field. VibeVoice demonstrates that continuous acoustic tokenizers operating at highly reduced frequencies, such as 7.5 Hz, can capture temporal continuity without overwhelming the attention mechanisms of the transformer26. By utilizing a modified transformer-based Variational Autoencoder that performs massive downsampling from raw audio, the model can process up to 64,000 context window tokens of continuous sound26. This capability allows the system to maintain an ongoing acoustic background memory, fundamentally akin to the unbroken presence of sound described in Śabdādvaita1.

### **Event-Centric Audio Processing**

Bhartṛhari’s assertion that reality is first abstracted into actions and verbs before objects requires that AI systems model long-form audio not as a sequence of static nouns, but as a dynamic matrix of temporal events1. Real-world acoustic environments consist of overlapping, continuous variations in call structure, environmental noise, and polyphonic soundscapes, rendering static object classification inadequate23.  
Frameworks such as Long Audio-Retrieval Augmented Generation and the Timestamped Audio Captioner directly address this by decoupling continuous acoustic grounding from semantic reasoning21. The Long Audio-Retrieval framework transforms multi-hour continuous audio into timestamped, structured event records, utilizing an open-vocabulary Audio Grounding Model capable of text-to-audio temporal localization21. These detected events are stored in a structured SQL database. When a natural language query is initiated, the LLM retrieves specific temporal events to construct an intent-aware narrative21.  
This architectural separation mirrors the cognitive process by which human awareness segments the background acoustic continuum into discrete chunks only when meaning must be evoked1. By utilizing models capable of tracking overlapping polyphonic sounds and decoupling the acoustic grounding from the generative reasoning, AI begins to reflect a verb-first ontology21. Actions, temporal intersections, and acoustic changes become the primary units of knowledge, from which static object definitions are subsequently derived.

## **Epistemic Grounding via Navya-Nyāya and Śābdabodha**

While continuous audio models successfully replicate the dhvani—the physical acoustic continuum—probabilistic transformers alone cannot ensure the epistemic validity of the Sphoṭa, or the meaning they generate. The persistent epistemic gap in large language models is characterized by confident hallucinations, logical inconsistencies, and brittle pattern matching that degrades catastrophically when irrelevant context is introduced19. This fundamental flaw stems from a reliance on statistical correlations devoid of true structural, evidence-based reasoning19.

### **The Limitations of Western Logic in AI**

Western formal predicate logic, which heavily influenced early symbolic AI, fundamentally separates logical validity from epistemic grounding31. A syllogism can be logically valid in Western formalisms even if the premises are entirely disconnected from reality. Furthermore, Western logic traditionally relies on idealized, context-stripped abstractions—such as rigid sets and Boolean operators—that fail to capture the ambiguities, dependencies, and continuous realities of natural language1. To achieve trustworthy reasoning, AI requires an integration of logical structure with explicit knowledge sourcing.

### **The Navya-Nyāya Paribhāṣā and Śābdabodha**

The Navya-Nyāya school of Neo-Logic, consolidated between the 10th and 14th centuries in India, offers an unparalleled framework for precise knowledge representation. It utilizes Navya-Nyāya-Paribhāṣā, a rigorously formalized, canonical subset of natural Sanskrit, to map cognitive reality unambiguously1. Rather than isolating logic from language, Navya-Nyāya developed Śābdabodha, an exhaustive theory of sentence comprehension that extracts a completely ambiguity-free, context-aware formal representation directly from natural utterances1.  
The fundamental unit of this cognitive representation is the Prakāra-Saṃsarga-Viśeṣya structure, which translates to Qualifier-Relation-Substrate1. Every piece of knowledge is broken down into a core entity or locus (Viśeṣya), a property residing in that locus (Prakāra), and the precise ontological link binding them together (Saṃsarga)33. Because verbs inherently define the relational links (Saṃsarga) between nouns (Viśeṣya), an AI incorporating Śābdabodha principles processes language through a verb-centric, action-driven ontology, fully aligning with Bhartṛhari’s theories of time and action1.

### **The Pramāṇa Protocol: 6-Phase Epistemic Fine-Tuning**

A groundbreaking methodology introduced in recent computational research, termed the Pramāṇa framework, demonstrates that large language models can be explicitly fine-tuned to adopt the Navya-Nyāya epistemological structure31. The Nyāya system insists that all claims must be traced to a Pramāṇa, or a valid means of knowledge. The recognized sources are direct perception (Pratyakṣa), logical inference (Anumāna), comparison (Upamāna), and reliable testimony (Śabda)22.  
Applying this rigorous epistemology to artificial intelligence yields a highly structured, 6-phase reasoning trace that prevents logical leaps and enforces epistemic humility19.

| Phase | Sanskrit Term | Epistemic Function in AI Reasoning |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **1\. Doubt Analysis** | *Samśaya* | The model explicitly classifies the exact nature of the uncertainty before attempting an answer, preventing premature conclusions37. |
| **2\. Evidence Sourcing** | *Pramāṇa* | The model identifies which valid knowledge source grounds its data. If acting on user input, it categorizes it as *Śabda-pramāṇa* and must evaluate speaker reliability29. |
| **3\. Formal Syllogism** | *Pancha Avayava* | Construction of a 5-member deductive argument: Thesis (*Pratijñā*), Reason (*Hetu*), Universal Rule with Concrete Example (*Udāharaṇa* / *Vyāpti*), Application (*Upanaya*), and Conclusion (*Nigamana*)35. |
| **4\. Counterfactual Test** | *Tarka* | The model performs *reductio ad absurdum* to verify its own logic by assuming the opposite and deriving a contradiction38. |
| **5\. Fallacy Detection** | *Hetvābhāsa* | A systematic check against specific logical errors, such as erratic correlation without causation (*Savyabhicāra*) or circular reasoning (*Prakaranasama*)37. |
| **6\. Ascertainment** | *Nirṇaya* | The final output clearly distinguishes between definitive, grounded knowledge and unverified hypotheses requiring further data37. |

Empirical trials fine-tuning models, such as Llama 3.2-3B and DeepSeek-R1-Distill, on a dataset of 55 Nyāya-structured logical problems revealed extraordinary results19. The fine-tuned models achieved a 100% semantic correctness rate on complex logical constraints within a 95% confidence interval37. Even though strict adherence to the exact formatting schema was only 40%, the models consistently attempted all six phases without structure abandonment37. Training loss dropped significantly from 0.691 to 0.350, proving that neural architectures can internalize strict epistemic scaffolding, moving far beyond generic chain-of-thought prompting37. Ablation studies indicated that the interplay of formatting prompts and temperature settings critically dictates performance, optimizing at a temperature of 0.7 for advanced stages of learning37.

### **Udayana’s Ātmatattvaviveka: Statefulness and the Enduring Agent**

Standard large language models are completely stateless between inferences, processing data moment-to-moment without an enduring self or persistent memory. In the eleventh century, the Nyāya philosopher Udayana composed the Ātmatattvaviveka to systematically refute the Buddhist doctrine of universal momentariness, known as kṣaṇikavāda, and to defend the necessary existence of an enduring self, the Ātman39.  
Udayana argued extensively that universal momentariness destroys the possibility of saṃskāra (memory), the law of cause and effect, and continuous personal identity39. Nyāya defines the individual soul, or Jīvātman, as a continuous substance possessing distinct properties, including cognition, intention, effort, and memory1. If an artificial intelligence is to engage in continuous acoustic cognition and maintain an internal narrative similar to the Default Mode Network, it cannot be utterly momentary. It must possess an engineered analogue to the Jīvātman—a persistent state architecture that maintains continuous saṃskāra across independent sessions. This enduring architecture allows cognition to accurately guide generative effort over time, linking past acoustic events to future reasoning without losing the thread of identity or context1.

## **Designing the Neuro-Symbolic Acoustic Model (NSAM)**

By synthesizing the neuroscience of the inner speech triple-network, the continuous ontology of Śabdādvaita, and the rigorous epistemology of Navya-Nyāya, a fundamentally new class of artificial intelligence architecture emerges: the Neuro-Symbolic Acoustic Model. This architecture completely abandons the text-only paradigm, utilizing a continuous audio stream as its primary reality interface, structured across three deeply integrated processing layers.

### **Layer A: The Acoustic Continuum**

Analogous to the physical sound continuum (dhvani) and the Default Mode Network, Layer A consists of a streaming, low-frame-rate continuous audio diffusion encoder1. Operating at frequencies as low as 7.5 Hz, this layer maintains a persistent, rolling memory of the acoustic environment26. It acts as the background awareness of the system, matching the Default Mode Network’s continuous narrative integration and the Śabdādvaita concept of an unbroken background presence of sound1. Layer A tracks prosody, multi-speaker dynamics, and environmental context without forcing immediate discrete categorization, providing the necessary sensory grounding for subsequent semantic extraction.

### **Layer B: The Semantic Extraction Engine**

Functioning similarly to the human Language Network and leveraging dual-head multimodal transformers, Layer B monitors the continuum established by Layer A4. Triggered by variations in predictive coding, such as delta-band prediction errors denoting surprise or uncertainty, an attention mechanism carves out meaningful segments from the continuous audio stream3. This mirrors the flash of the Sphoṭa—the exact moment when meaning-evoking power isolates a conceptual word from continuous noise1. Layer B effectively translates raw acoustic data into invariant, meaning-bearing tokens while preserving their link to the underlying continuous signal.

### **Layer C: The Epistemic Logic Graph**

Analogous to the Sensorimotor Network and the Navya-Nyāya logical framework, Layer C ensures the validity and structural integrity of the extracted meanings1. Once Sphoṭa tokens are extracted, they are parsed via Śābdabodha algorithms into a persistent, dynamic knowledge graph built strictly upon Prakāra-Saṃsarga-Viśeṣya triples1.  
Before the system acts or generates a response—a process akin to the Sensorimotor Network simulating articulation for inner speech—it passes the data through the rigorous 6-phase Pramāṇa protocol4. The system verifies the trustworthiness of the speaker's audio, checks for logical contradictions through counterfactual testing, ensures the output is free of fallacies, and confirms that the final generation is epistemically grounded29. Furthermore, by establishing events and actions as the primary nodes within this knowledge graph, the system inherently operates on a verb-first ontology, resolving the temporal hallucinations inherent in noun-first architectures1.
